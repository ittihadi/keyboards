We begin our story around the start of 2025, around this time I was in my keyboard
modding phase and had just finished lubricating my keyboard's stock switches. Then
at the beginning of April YouTube decided to show me a video on the
[ZSA Voyager](https://www.zsa.io/voyager). This was my first exposure to split
keyboards and I was interested in the concept of it to say the least.

Coincidentally not long after that the topic of proper typing form was brought up at
uni and I decided to bring up the Voyager to my classmates as a counter argument to
the view that the standard touch type QWERTY method was the only way to properly type.

At the time I was mostly just joking, but in the end I decided to try finding out more
about the Voyager and other split keyboards, because I wanted one, but there was no
way I can afford just buying the Voyager itself.

A quick web search later, I found a nice
[list](https://www.reddit.com/r/ErgoMechKeyboards/comments/17fnxe4)
of potential boards that I can consider. I was thinking at first that I can just buy
a preexisting board, but after glancing at the prices for them I decided to try and
find one I could build, I mean, how hard can it be, right?

I took a look at the [piantor](https://github.com/beekeeb/piantor), the
[sofle](https://josefadamcik.github.io/SofleKeyboard/), the
[corne](https://github.com/foostan/crkbd/), the
[lily58](https://github.com/kata0510/Lily58), and some others. In the end, I chose to
go with a modified Corne v1.
